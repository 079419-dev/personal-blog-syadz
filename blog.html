My Journey Into Blender: From Zero to 3D Models

When I first opened Blender, I was completely lost. The interface was intimidating — buttons everywhere, strange keyboard shortcuts, and a default cube staring at me. Here's how I turned confusion into creativity...

⏱ 5 min read

Starting with Blender felt overwhelming, but I quickly learned that every expert was once a beginner. My first project was a simple coffee cup — basic geometry, but it taught me the fundamentals of mesh editing, materials, and lighting.

I spent hours on YouTube tutorials and UniSZA lab sessions, slowly understanding how to manipulate vertices, edges, and faces. The "G" key to grab, "R" to rotate, "S" to scale — these shortcuts became second nature.

My biggest breakthrough came when I rendered my first 3D scene with realistic lighting. That moment of seeing a flat mesh transform into something that looked three-dimensional and real — it was addictive. Now I use Blender for every academic project that requires visual representation.

Tips for Blender beginners: Start with the donut tutorial by Blender Guru, practice shortcuts daily, and don't be afraid to make mistakes — that's how you learn.

Game Dev ◆ 2 April 2025

Building My First Interactive App in Unity 3D

Unity 3D opened a completely new world for me — the world of interactive experiences. Creating something a user can actually click, move, and interact with is a completely different feeling from static design...

⏱ 4 min read

My introduction to Unity 3D was through my multimedia courseware Final Year Project. The goal was to create an interactive educational application — and Unity was the perfect tool for it.

I learned C# basics to write simple scripts: button interactions, scene transitions, and basic animations. The concept of GameObjects and Components was confusing at first, but once it clicked, everything made sense.

The most rewarding part was seeing someone actually use my app and navigate through it. That user-first thinking changed how I approach all my projects now.

What I learned: User experience matters more than fancy features. Simple, clean interactions always win.

Graphic Design ◆ 10 March 2025

What Adobe Creative Suite Taught Me About Storytelling

Design isn't just about making things look pretty — it's about telling a story without words. Every color choice, font selection, and layout decision communicates something to the viewer...

⏱ 6 min read

Working with Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign has fundamentally changed how I see the world around me. I now notice kerning in restaurant menus, hierarchy in billboard designs, and color psychology in brand logos.

Designing posters for academic events taught me to ask: Who is the audience? What is the ONE message they must take away? How do I guide their eye through the composition?

My favorite Adobe tool is Illustrator — vector graphics that scale infinitely and the pen tool that, once mastered, feels like a superpower. I've used it to create logos, infographics, and complete visual identity systems.

Design principle I live by: Less is more. Every element must earn its place in the design.
